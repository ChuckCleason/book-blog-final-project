The Catcher in the Rye

The Catcher in the Rye by J. D. Salinger is about a teenage boy named Holden who struggles with depression and the world he lives in. He desides to run away from his school and take an adventure in the city where he meets different people and do new things that help him put his world into perspective. 4.5 stars

The Great Gatsby

The Great Gatsby by Scott Fitzgerald is in the perspectiv of Nick Caroway and his experiences with his neighbor Jay Gatsby. As a new stock broker who comes from a rich family, he spends much of his time trying to figure out his rich neighbor who is in love with his cosin. While find out the many secerets of Gatsby, Nick trys to figure out how to go about life. 3.5 stars

To Kill a Mockingbird

To Kill a Mockingbird by Harper Lee is a coming of age story about Scout Finch and how the harshness of the world comes into view for her. Her father, a lawer, has to defend a man who has no chance of wining even thousgh he did nothing wrong. She meets the misterious neighbor who has to stay in his house all day since he is not considered socially accepable. She watchs as the poor kids in her class can't bring lunch to school because they can't afford food. Through these experiences, and with the help of her older brother, Jem, and her father, Aticus, Scout learns how to deal with the world and the harsh realities of growing up. 4.5 stars

A Northern Light

A Norhtern Light by Jennifer Donnelly is about a farm girl who wants to go to college but knows her father would never aprove of help her get there. In order to pay, she get a job at the local hotel with her best friend who plans to go with her. But as a local, higher up, farm boy starts to show more interest in her, her motavarions and intentions start to change. As her teacher moves away and her famiily getting sick and losing there best cow, her options also start to change. 4 stars
